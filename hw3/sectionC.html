Caustics

From the video we saw last week, it appears to me that caustics work by mapping a texture to transparent geometry.
That texture redfracts the ray particles as they hit the geometry, and project a distorted caustic on the next geometry the light probes hit.



This is an image of a real-life caustic filtered by a glass cup. The light
refraction does not need to be accurate to look like a high-definition caustic.
Sometimes, a graphics engineer wants the caustic to be completely accurate.
In this case, complex techniques like photon mapping are used for maximum fidelity.
It is my impression that caustics won't be generated on a surface where there is
no direct path from light source, to the transparent object, to the surface.
Caustics are rendered perpendicular to the light source, so that orbiting the light
around the gemotry rotates the caustic around the caustic around the geometry.



Here we see a computer generated glass cup and caustic. It has been rendered with photon mapping to achieve sufficient fidelity.
